# OPUS — Identity & Brand

---

## The Name

**OPUS** carries a double meaning:
- **Latin:** "work" — as in a body of work, a life's creative output, opus magnum
- **Romanian:** "opus" = "opposite" — doing things differently, against the grain

Both meanings are intentional. OPUS is about the work of making things, and about making things the opposite way from how the industry does it.

## The Tagline

**"Human experience design"**

Not user experience. Not customer experience. Human experience. The distinction matters: OPUS designs for people as whole beings — their attention, their relationships, their calm, their creativity — not just for people as users of a product.

## The Founder

- Background in software engineering, career in UX/UI design

The personal story feeds the brand: someone who spent 15+ years designing other people's products, now finally building his own. The transition from freelance grind to founder is the company's origin story.

## The Visual Identity

- **Dark theme** — iOS 26-inspired UI aesthetic
- **Octopus** — Brand mascot concept: "cries and does multiple things at once." Honest, multitasking, emotional. A perfect metaphor for the indie maker life.
- **Ferrofluid** — Visual exploration of ferrofluid physics and sound-reactive visuals. Potential for app animations, brand imagery, and the connection between sound and form (relevant to Opus Loop).

## Brand Voice

Honest. Direct. A little sarcastic. Never corporate. OPUS talks like a person, not a company. The CyniCal notification copy is the purest expression of this — guilt-tripping you into being better while making you laugh.

The brand doesn't take itself too seriously, but it takes its craft very seriously. Premium quality, human tone.

## The "From 2042" Concept

From the Make Future notes: "Opus.ro as a website from 2042." The idea that OPUS designs products for a future that doesn't exist yet — imagining better things and building them now. Not trend-following. Future-making.

---

## What OPUS Is Not

- Not a design agency (though it offers design services to fund product development)
- Not a startup chasing venture capital (bootstrapped, sustainable)
- Not a content company (no courses, no guides, no "10 tips for better meditation")
- Not trying to be big (trying to be good)
