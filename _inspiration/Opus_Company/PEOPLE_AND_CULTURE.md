# OPUS — People, Culture & MakeFuture

---

## The Founder's Journey

Cata's path to OPUS wasn't a straight line from school to startup. It was 15+ years of freelance iOS design — learning the craft by building other people's products, developing deep platform expertise, working with international clients (primarily American and Scandinavian), and slowly accumulating the skill, taste, and frustration needed to finally build his own things.

The pivot from freelancer to founder isn't just a career move. It's the central narrative: "I've been designing products for everyone else. Now I'm making the ones I actually believe in."

### Opus Collective Model

Rather than hiring, OPUS uses a collective model for collaborations:
- Creator-first revenue split (100% to creators)
- OPUS funded through Patreon/donations, not by extracting from collaborators
- Project-based engagements (LOOP with DNT/IRD, ACCEPTTHECOOKIE with IRD)
- Philosophy: OPUS provides the platform and coordination; creators keep their work and their money

This reflects OPUS's broader values — decentralization, fair compensation, and skepticism of extraction-based business models.

## Culture & Values

### How OPUS Works

**Remote-first, Romania-based.** Cluj-Napoca as home base, but work happens wherever the person is.

**Craft over speed.** Products ship when they're good, not when the sprint ends. Quality is the only deadline that matters.

**Honesty as design principle.** CyniCal's sarcastic notifications are the purest expression: don't sugarcoat, don't corporate-speak, just be real.

**Sustainability over growth.** No VC money means no pressure to scale at all costs. Products can be small and good. Revenue needs to cover costs and fund the next thing, not generate hockey-stick charts for investors.

**Personal practice feeds product.** The 21-day meditation journal (abundance.md) isn't separate from the work — it IS the work. You can't design tools for human wellbeing if you're not doing the inner work yourself.

### The Anti-Corporate Position

OPUS exists in deliberate opposition to:
- Subscription exploitation (charge once, respect the customer)
- Surveillance capitalism (no tracking, no analytics, no selling data)
- Algorithmic manipulation (no feeds, no engagement optimization)
- Growth-at-all-costs culture (small and good > big and compromised)

This isn't just branding — it's the structural decision that shapes every product choice. No backend means no data to sell. No subscription means no incentive to create artificial dependency. No VC means no pressure to betray users for returns.
