# OPUS — Business & Revenue

---

## Revenue Model

### Products: One-Time Purchase

OPUS products follow a premium-but-fair pricing model:
- **Opus Loop:** $4.99 one-time purchase
- **CyniCal:** TBD (likely similar price point)
- **Future products:** Same philosophy — pay once, own forever

No subscriptions. No in-app purchases for core features. Optional expansions (sound packs, themes) possible but never required.

### Services: Freelance Design

The primary revenue source currently:
- iOS app design at $70/hour
- Equity flexibility for aligned projects
- Primarily American and Scandinavian clients
- 15+ years of client relationships and reputation

This is the bridge: service revenue funds product development until products sustain themselves.

### Alternative Revenue Concepts

**Patreon / Fan Support:** Early OPUS concept included supporter-funded model — digital assets for supporters, 100% of collaboration revenue going to creators with OPUS funded by donations. This reflects a preference for patronage over transactional relationships.

## Growth Strategy

### No VC, No Rush

OPUS is deliberately bootstrapped. The strategy:
1. Service revenue provides steady income
2. Products are built in parallel, using the service income as runway
3. Each product is designed to sustain itself at modest scale (thousands of users, not millions)
4. No growth-at-all-costs mentality

### Market Positioning

OPUS occupies the "indie premium" space:
- Higher quality than free alternatives
- More honest than subscription competitors
- More personal than corporate products
- "Made in Romania by OPUS" — the indie maker story as differentiator

### Key Launch Moment

iOS 26 / iPhone 17 release cycle:
- Co-launch Opus Loop and CyniCal
- Leverage Apple's new Liquid UI design language
- Target Apple Design Awards
- Paul handles Loop development, Cata handles CyniCal
- Shared marketing moment establishes OPUS as a multi-product brand
