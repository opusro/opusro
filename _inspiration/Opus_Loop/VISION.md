# Opus Loop — Vision & Origins

---

## The Personal Story

Opus Loop didn't start as a product strategy. It started with a heart attack.

In 2020, Cata's father — the family's protector, the steady center — had a heart attack. This was followed by a car accident that shook the family further. These events cracked open the question that meditation practice is supposed to help with: how do you stay present when the ground shifts under you?

The meditation practice that followed wasn't casual. It was survival. And the apps available for it were either bloated with content you didn't need, locked behind subscriptions you resented, or designed for beginners when what you needed was a simple, reliable tool.

Opus Loop was built to fill that gap — for Cata first, and then for anyone who's been meditating long enough to know they just need a timer, a sound, and silence.

---

## The Philosophy

### Against Subscription Meditation

The meditation app market is dominated by subscription models: Calm, Headspace, Insight Timer (freemium). They make money by keeping you engaged — more content, more courses, more features. The incentive is to make meditation feel like something you can't do without their guidance.

Opus Loop rejects this entirely:
- **One-time purchase** ($4.99). You buy it once. It's yours.
- **No content library.** No guided meditations, no courses, no celebrity narrators.
- **No tracking pressure.** No streaks, no gamification, no "you meditated 47 days in a row!" dopamine hits.
- **Your sounds, your way.** Upload your own mp3s or use Apple Music integration.

The product position: if you already know how to meditate, you don't need an app that teaches you. You need a tool that gets out of your way.

### The OPUS Meaning

OPUS means "work" in Latin. In Romanian, it means "opposite." Both meanings are baked into the product: the work of sitting still, and the opposite of what every other meditation app does.

The deeper philosophy: art as work, work as art. Creating things for beauty and meaning, not just for market fit. Making products that serve the future — "Opus.ro as a website from 2042."

## Core Concept

A meditation timer with:
- **Duration picker** — set your session length
- **Background sounds** — ambient audio during the session
- **Gong** — configurable interval bell (halfway, custom intervals)
- **Continue playback** — option to keep sound going after timer ends
- **Fade control** — smooth audio transitions

Three modes:
1. **Meditation** — The core. Timer + sound + gong.
2. **Pomodoro / Deep Work** — Work timer with focus sounds. Same engine, different intent.
3. **Custom** — Physical activity, yoga, stretching. User-defined setups.

### What Makes It Different

**Custom mp3 uploads.** Most meditation apps lock you into their library. Loop lets you upload any sound — rain recordings from your own backyard, a specific singing bowl you love, binaural beats you've been using for years.

**Apple Music integration.** Play from your library. No separate ecosystem.

**No bloat.** The feature set is deliberately minimal. There's nothing to figure out. Open the app, set a time, pick a sound, start.

**Privacy.** No account, no tracking, no analytics on your meditation habits. Local data + iCloud sync. That's it.

## The Broader Vision

Opus Loop is the first OPUS product — the proof that the philosophy works. If it succeeds, it validates the approach: premium quality, one-time purchase, no surveillance, designed for people who know what they want.

It's also meant to fund the next steps: CyniCal development, the broader OPUS product ecosystem, and eventually the Make Future foundation.

From the commitment journal: after 15+ years of freelance grind, Opus Loop represents the pivot — from designing other people's products to shipping your own. The meditation app isn't just a product; it's the first act of a new chapter.
