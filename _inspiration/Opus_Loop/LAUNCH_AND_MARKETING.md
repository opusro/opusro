## Marketing Strategy

### Approach: Organic, Personal, Budget-Conscious

No paid ads (at least not initially). The strategy is authenticity over reach:

1. **Personal story** — The heart attack, the meditation practice, the frustration with existing apps. This story IS the marketing.
2. **YouTube channel** — App walkthrough, meditation philosophy, behind-the-scenes of building an indie app.
3. **Anti-subscription positioning** — Every frustrated Calm/Headspace user is a potential customer. "You already know how to meditate. Stop paying someone to tell you that." This angle generates organic conversation.
4. **Design community** — Cata's 16-year design background and iOS expertise lend credibility. Product Hunt, design Twitter/Bluesky, indie maker communities.

### YouTube Launch Strategy

- Critique of subscription meditation apps (why they're misaligned)
- App demo: "Here's what $4.99 gets you, forever"
- Personal story: why this app exists
- Technical breakdown: Apple vibe coding, SwiftUI, indie development

### Loop Day — Live Event Concept

A day-long event combining meditation and music:
- Hourly meditation loops alternating with ecstatic dance loops
- Multiple performers and artists
- Overhead filming for video backgrounds (reusable as app content)
- Community building through shared practice

This is marketing-as-experience: people don't just hear about the app, they experience the philosophy.

## Growth Model

### Organic Loops (no pun intended)

1. **Anti-subscription converts** — People actively searching for one-time-purchase meditation apps
2. **Apple Music users** — People who want their own music during meditation (underserved niche)
3. **Custom sound uploaders** — Sound healers, specific tradition practitioners, binaural beat enthusiasts
4. **Pomodoro crossover** — Productivity people who also meditate (significant overlap)
5. **Indie app supporters** — People who deliberately choose indie over corporate

### Future Revenue

- App sales (primary)
- Potential sound pack expansions (optional, not required for core experience)
- AI-generated soundscapes (if the concept proves viable)

## Context: Why This Matters for OPUS

Opus Loop is the first shipped OPUS product. Its success or failure sets the trajectory:

- **If it works:** Validates the one-time-purchase, privacy-first, quality-over-growth model. Funds CyniCal development. Proves OPUS can ship.
- **Revenue target context:** From the goals/budget notes, Opus Loop had an allocated €6,000 development budget, suggesting it needs to earn that back + fund next steps.
- **IRD UBI support:** Development was partially supported by UBI from IRD SRL in 2025, which enabled a peaceful development pace and the achievement of the launch milestone.
